# Chapter 4: Gear and Stats

Understanding your diving habits and equipment history is an essential part of the digital logbook. Pelagic Desktop provides rich tools to manage your gear and visualize your dives over time.

## 1. Managing your Equipment

Keep track of your regulators, BCDs, dive computers, tanks, wetsuits, and camera setups.

Access your **Equipment** dashboard from the Global Header.

1. Click on **Add Equipment** and the Equipment Modal will appear.
2. Enter the details: Manufacturer, Model, Serial Number, Date Acquired, and Type.
3. Keep track of service dates and notes.

![Equipment Dashboard](./screenshots/equipment-dashboard.png)
*Take a screenshot of the main Equipment view listing gear and save it as `equipment-dashboard.png`.*

Within individual dives you can associate your defined equipment list.
- **Why log gear?** This helps you understand how different wetsuits affect your weighting requirements, or when a specific regulator is due for service based on the number of hours it's been down.

![Equipment Assignment Modal](./screenshots/equipment-assignment.png)
*Take a screenshot of the Equipment Modal assigning gear to a specific dive. Save as `equipment-assignment.png`.*

## 2. Viewing your Statistics Dashboard

If you want the big picture, the **Statistics** section provides interactive logs of your diving history.

1. Open the **Statistics** page from the main Global Header.
2. The interactive **StatsBar** and charts visualize metrics such as:
   - Total number of dives
   - Total time underwater
   - Maximum depths and distributions
   - Average temperatures you dive in most frequently

![Statistics Hub](./screenshots/statistics-hub.png)
*Save a screenshot of the main Statistics dashboards and charts as `statistics-hub.png`.*

### In-Depth Dive Profiles

Beyond global statistics, selecting a single dive log will show you the exact **Dive Profile**: A granular chart showing your depth and temperature over the duration of the dive.

- Click on any point on the profile graph to see the exact time and depth.
- If your dive computer records advanced telemetry (like tank pressure, deco ceilings, or heart rate), it will be overlaid here.
