# Chapter 5: Community and Exporting

The Pelagic Desktop app is a robust personal logbook, but it also allows you to interact with global diving data and share your experiences. This chapter wraps up the tutorial with how to collaborate and export your logs.

## 1. Exploring the Community Map

To discover new dive sites or see where others are logging dives:

1. Click on the **Community** button in the Global Header.
2. The Community Map interface will open showing a global view of shared dive sites.
3. You can click on pins to view site details, aggregated marine species sightings, and general conditions.
4. Interact with the **Community Modal** to share your own confirmed dive site coordinates or download popular sites to your local database before a trip.

## 2. Generating Share Cards

Want to quickly share the highlight of a dive on social media without exporting full CSV data?

1. Open a **Dive** or a stunning **Photo**.
2. Look for the Share icon or right-click context menu.
3. The **Share Card Modal** allows you to generate a beautifully formatted graphic featuring:
   - Your selected cover photo
   - Key dive statistics (Max Depth, Location, Time)
   - Verified species tags

![Share Card Generator](./screenshots/share-card.png)
*Take a screenshot of the Share Card Modal generating an image for social sharing. Save as `share-card.png`.*

## 3. Exporting Data and Profiles

To ensure your data remains completely yours to control, Pelagic offers comprehensive export tools.

1. Navigate to the **Export** section from the Global Header, or use the **Context Menu** on a specific Trip.
2. The Export Modal provides multiple formats:
   - **CSV / Spreadsheet:** For raw data analysis of dive logs.
   - **Pelagic Backup format:** A total backup of your local database.
   - **Printable PDFs:** Generate an old-school paper logbook directly from your imported dive data.

![Export Options Modal](./screenshots/export-modal.png)

## 4. Global Search

As your logbook grows to hundreds of dives, finding that one specific time you saw a Manta Ray becomes difficult.

1. Press `Ctrl+K` (or `Cmd+K` on Mac) anywhere in the app, or click the **Search** bar in the Header.
2. The **Search Modal** appears.
3. Type keywords related to:
   - Dive sites (e.g., "Manta Point")
   - Species Tags
   - Dive Notes
   - Locations

The search results update instantly, providing quick links directly to the specific dive or photo.
