# Chapter 2: Importing and Managing Dives

At the core of Pelagic Desktop is your digital logbook. This chapter explains how to bring your dive data into the app and organize it perfectly.

## 1. Direct Dive Computer Imports

You can import extensive dive histories directly from your supported dive computers via a USB connection or Bluetooth.

1. Click on the **Import Dives** button located in the Global Header.
2. The Dive Computer Modal will appear. Select your computer manufacturer and model from the list.
3. Follow the on-screen instructions to connect and sync your logs.
4. Review the incoming data before confirming.

## 2. Manual Dive Additions

Don't have a supported dive computer or just want to manually add an old log? No problem.

1. In the **Sidebar**, look for the prominent **Add manual dive** action.
2. A new dive entry form will pop up, allowing you to manually input:
   - Date, Time, and Duration
   - Max Depth and Temperatures
   - Gas Mixes and Start/End Pressures
   - Narrative Notes

![Add Manual Dive Form](./screenshots/add-manual-dive.png)

## 3. Organizing into Trips

Keeping dives organized chronologically and by location is easy with the concept of **Trips**.

- **Creating a Trip:** Click **New Trip** in the Sidebar. Give it a name (e.g., "Bonaire 2026"), specify the location, resort, and the start/end dates.
- **Adding Dives to Trips:** You can drag and drop imported or manual dives into specific trips via the Sidebar.
- **Editing Trips:** A quick right-click (or double-click) on a Trip in the Sidebar opens a context menu giving you options to edit details or rename the trip entirely.

![Trip Organization in Sidebar](./screenshots/trip-organization.png)

## 4. Bulk Edits

Need to change the location or tags for an entire week's worth of dives?

1. Select multiple dives in your Trip view using a selection box or standard multi-select commands (Shift-Click/Ctrl-Click).
2. Right-click and choose **Bulk Edit**.
3. Use the Batch Operations Modal to update metadata across all selected dives simultaneously.
