# Chapter 1: Getting Started

Welcome to Pelagic Desktop! This chapter covers the initial setup and familiarizes you with the main interface.

## 1. The Setup Wizard

When you first launch Pelagic, you will be greeted by the Setup Wizard. This helps you establish your primary settings, configure initial options, and optionally import an existing database.

![Setup Wizard Screen](./screenshots/setup-wizard.png)

## 2. Navigating the App Layout

The app is divided into three primary zones to keep your workflow organized:

### The Global Header
At the top of the app, you will find the Global Header. This is your command center for primary actions:
- **Search (Ctrl+K/Cmd+K):** Instantly find dives, sites, or photos.
- **Import:** Add new logs from a dive computer or photos from a folder.
- **Top-Level Views:** Switch between your Map, Statistics, Community, and Equipment.

![Main App Header](./screenshots/main-header.png)
*Save a screenshot of the top header bar as `main-header.png`.*

### The Sidebar Navigation
Located on the left, the collapsible Sidebar manages your digital logbook.
- **Trips:** Your highest level of organization.
- **Dives:** Nested under trips, you can see specific dives with basic metrics (like max depth).
- **Actions:** Add new trips or manual dives directly from here.

![Sidebar Navigation](./screenshots/sidebar-nav.png)
*Save a screenshot of the expanded sidebar with trips/dives as `sidebar-nav.png`.*

### The Content Area
The large central workspace updates dynamically based on what you select in the Sidebar or Header.
- Selecting a trip shows a summary grid of all photos from that trip.
- Selecting a dive displays the detailed dive profile, dive site info, and attached photos.

![Content Area Grid](./screenshots/content-area.png)
*Save a screenshot of the central content grid populated with photos as `content-area.png`.*

## 3. Settings

To customize Pelagic Desktop further, click the **Settings** gear icon in the header.

Here you can manage:
- App preferences and UI themes.
- Language and unit preferences (Metric/Imperial).
- API Keys (e.g., configuring Gemini for AI tagging or smart features).
